### 1. Tell me about a time when you faced a disagreement within a team. How did you handle the conflict, and what was the outcome?

**Situation:**  
During my Applied AI internship at Gusto, I was part of a small team developing a machine learning model to predict customer churn. Midway through, the data scientist and I disagreed on the feature selection approach. He favored a traditional statistical method, while I believed a more modern embedding technique would yield better results.

**Task:**  
Our goal was to reach consensus on the feature engineering strategy, as it would ultimately impact the model’s accuracy and the project timeline. It was important to reconcile differing opinions quickly to maintain momentum.

**Action:**  
I initiated a one-on-one discussion with my teammate to understand his reasoning fully, listening actively to his concerns about model interpretability and resource constraints. Then, I shared my perspective, backing it up with recent research and example cases where embeddings improved predictive power. I proposed a quick A/B experiment to evaluate both methods with our dataset, which would allow data-driven decision-making. We agreed to this, keeping the team informed of the plan and results.

**Result:**  
The experiment showed the embedding approach improved accuracy by 8% without significantly increasing latency. The team adopted my method, and our model ultimately contributed to a 5% reduction in churn during the pilot phase. This experience taught me the value of empathy, collaborative problem-solving, and humility in resolving conflicts to achieve shared success.

### 5. Describe a moment when you faced ambiguity or incomplete information in a project. How did you navigate the uncertainty?

**Situation:**  
While working on an AI-powered payroll prediction model during my Gusto internship, I encountered incomplete historical data and unclear business definitions around certain compensation categories.

**Task:**  
Despite these gaps, the project needed a viable prototype delivered within six weeks to support strategic planning.

**Action:**  
I began by listing knowns versus unknowns and prioritized clarifying the most critical data gaps through discussions with payroll subject matter experts. For ambiguous definitions, I proposed provisional assumptions documented transparently. I designed the model to be modular so that refining assumptions later would be straightforward. Meanwhile, I performed exploratory data analysis to identify patterns and leveraged synthetic data augmentation to compensate for missing data points. I kept stakeholders informed regularly to manage expectations.

**Result:**  
We delivered an initial model that provided actionable insights and identified key data quality areas for future improvement. This taught me how to stay proactive and adaptable, balancing speed with rigor amid uncertainty.
